Using behavioral economics to nudge users to to get things done.

Sep - Dec 2020

Behavior theories

Goal setting, Priming, Default bias, Framing

Problem

People are constantly distracted from important tasks or moments when working from home. So, tasks are not accomplished in time.

According to a survey based on over 2300 respondents in 2020 June, using social media (61%) and smartphones (53%) are ranked as two of the highest distractions for working-from-home professionals. Binge-watching is reported as the third, with 42% of respondents.

Solution

An app that nudges people to maintain and start to focus on getting things done.

1. Turn calendar events into a focus timer to easily stick to the schedule

Behaviorial economics

  • Integrate existing goals and resources: Google Calendar or Microsoft Outlook.
  • Making stick-to-a-goal easier
    •

    2. Break difficult tasks into smaller tasks

    Goal setting theory

  • Small wins and short-term goals are easier to focus on.
  • Subgoals feel more attainable than bigger goals.
    •

    3. Create a calendar event

    Behaviorial economics

  • An all-in-one place to schedule.
  • Making the better choice more cognitively available by placing turning on focus timer by default.
    •

    4. Start a focus timer any time and block the phone

    Behaviorial economics

  • Decrease the efforts needed to enter a focusing space.
  • Making the better choice more cognitively available by placing the call-to-action on the homepage.

    Insights

    User Validation

    The design needs to strike a balance between flexible breaks and focus sessions.

    With the first version of wireframe, I conducted remote usability testing sessions via Zoom with 4 users.

    Medium-fidelity User Validation

    Users want more focus timer embedded in default calendar settings.

    With the medium-fidelity mockup, I was able to ask for another round of feedback. To this point, the feedback I received is more specific for a component of a few features. For example, users want the focus timer being turned on when they initiate a calendar event from this app.

    Takeaway

    Nudging people into performing an ideal behavior is also a kind of user-centered design.

    My greatest takeaway from the project is the process of digging up psychological theories and behavior economics and intertwining them into the design. I feel a sense of achievement by knowing that this design is based on solid science and it will very possibly make a positive impact on users.
