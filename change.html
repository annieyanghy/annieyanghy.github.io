Change App - Sustainable Habit Forming


CHANGE helps you build sustainable habits in a gameful way so that you can make a change.

Problem

People face difficulties to do more sustainable actions in daily life despite being aware of the importance of sustainability to various degrees.

The origin of the project came from my passion for sustainability. I have done other projects regarding sustainability and feels like there are more needs to be done, and one of which is the daily sustainable behaviour. I have observed a serious problem happening daily ‚Äî the disposable eating utensils, which includes containers and cutlery. People eat out and use these disposable eating containers and other utensils automatically, without pausing and realizing that they can actually make a lot of change if they just bring a container to reuse. As a result, one of the goals of my master‚Äôs studies at the University of Michigan is to design a sustainable app that can help people develop sustainable habits, and I am also aiming to submit this project to CHI 2020 Student Game Competition.

Solution

An app that gamify habit forming process to motivate users take on more sustainable actions through the power of community and personal reflection.

How It Works

1. Group Sustainable Challenge

increase motivation by the sense of collaboration and competition

To increase the sense of community, we want to elevate the process of forming sustainable habits to a crowdsourcing game to not only motivate users to contribute to sustainable actions but also make them feel a sense of honor.

2. Community Map

to connect local users to form a community

With CHANGE, we build a local map to help users to connect to other users, so as to create a sense of belonging. Also, from our user research, we know that users are greatly influenced by the environment and the community, so we try to build this app with a map that can help users to find nearby like-minded people who are also interested in cultivating sustainable habits.

3. Personal Challenge

get rewards and reflection to internalize sustainable habits

Apart from group challenges, we also create the section for solo-challenge-takers. In personal challenges, we focus on reflection through self-rating and assessment. Also, to incentivize users, we provide small real-ward rewards to motivate them so that they know their reflection with CHANGE is greatly connected to their daily life.

APP Features

In the app, we designed two main features to help users to develop sustainable habits, one is Map and the other is Journey.

Design Process

Research

1. Observation & User Interview

To have a deeper understanding of people‚Äôs thoughts on sustainability and their current lifestyles, we interviewed 10 people that represented different stages of life, academic focuses, and cultural backgrounds. The interviewees include 1 Korean, 1 Chinese, 3 Taiwanese, and 5 Americans. All interviewees are between 20-50 years old and are currently living in the USA. However, most of them are young professionals or graduate students, who are a group of people we tried to target because they may still undergo a series of life changes, and might be more willing to develop new habits.

Before conducting the interviews, we developed interview protocols that focused on interviewees‚Äô lunch journeys with three different overarching questions:

  • What is preventing people from developing sustainable habits? (Group A)
  • How do people currently practice sustainable habits? (Group B)
  • What sustainable habits do you find easy and/or challenging to implement? (Group C)
    •

    We started each interview with a set of general questions inquiring the interviewee‚Äôs experience in practising sustainable habits. According to the response, we made a quick decision to place our interviewee into one of the following groups: people who are unfamiliar with the concept of sustainability and rarely practice sustainable habits (Group A), people who are fully aware and are actively practising sustainable habits (Group B), and those who are somewhere in-between (GroupC). The result led each interviewee to follow-up questions designed for specific groups.

  • What is preventing people from developing sustainable habits? (Group A)
  • How do people currently practice sustainable habits? (Group B)
  • What sustainable habits do you find easy and/or challenging to implement? (Group C)

    We started off by dividing the wireframes of the app to three parts, and each member of the team took a part in the app to develop the wireframes.     Design

    1. üí™üèº My solo task: Merge Design

    Then, after the wireframes are reviewed, I was responsible for merging the wireframe and put them into the high-fidelity design. Merging design is a considerable work, considering that everyone makes the design layout different and may use different paddings. As a result, to make the merging process efficient, I started by observing common components to create design components to be used in the whole app. After the design components were created, I could then used them in different app sections and easily edit them if I need a change. By starting off with design components, I can standardize the app design easily, mainly I need to merge design ideas from different people. At this stage, Figma helped greatly because I could take teammates‚Äô work easily ‚Äî through a shareable link! Also, I intensively used the newest featured released by Figma ‚Äî Auto Layout, when I was making components, which helped me to quickly take care of paddings and any change in design.


    2. üí™üèºMy solo task: Design System

    I used Atomic Design approach when merging the different wireframes. I started by making the smallest components, like texts or icons, and then combine them into larger components such as modals or cards.

    I used this approach to better systems the whole design styles, which also aids me to update multiple screens efficiently.
